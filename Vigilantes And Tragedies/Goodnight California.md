
### The Heroes

#### Anchal Lala / Kali
A Mighty Warrior who Needs No Weapon
Played by Jasmin

The most controversial member of the team, though at the time people only knew her as [[Kali]] and not her true name, [[Anchal|Anchal's]] story is quite mundane up until she receives bracelets from her grandmother that belonged to her mother. Upon wearing them, she became an avatar of Kali - the Hindu goddess - who would use her to project a form onto this Earth and wreak havoc upon the evil of the world. 

The first time that this happened, the rampage was stopped by another hero - known as [[Prester John]] - who lead Kali away from the more populated areas and struck her unconscious, revealing Anchal. She was then taken in by the [[Earth Defence Agency|EDA]], and after much testing and proving herself personally to Prester John, the [[Director Lane|Director]] and her [[Agent Delaware|handler]], she was given the go-ahead to join the Sentinel team.

This decision was made as she was a key asset that they couldn’t squander, having access to raw strength was a rarity after [[The Flash]]. Though there were a few moments where Kali might have pushed Anchal to make decisions she didn’t wish to, Anchal proved an excellent controller of the goddess - as far as one could call simply pushing back negotiating with a goddess control.

Anchal proved herself consistently to be the more human side of the group, having the view closest aligned with a civilian at times and bringing the party back down to Earth to remind them of their humanity. Though it is difficult to sum up Kali’s impression on the campaign in a way that doesn’t simply list the foes that she slew, it can be easily summed up by saying that in each great battle, she was present, and if she wasn’t, the Sentinels likely wouldn’t be where they are today. Her most notable kill is the King in Yellow himself, having struck the final blow.

Anchal grew to have a romance with a member of the [[Vandal Four]], and overall attempted to bring that team together and encourage them to be heroes. This eventually lead to her having them join her own team, the [[Arms of Kali]], proving further that Anchal’s presence in the story is often marked by moments of intense humanity or intense cosmic violence, as one would expect from the avatar of a god of war.

#### Hyejin "Jinny" Sampson
A Tongue-Tied Adept who Infiltrates
Played by Helene

[[Jinny|Jinny's]] backstory is tightly joined with [[Dread-Man|Dread-Man's]] and the [[Foreshadow Taskforce]]. A child of superhero-police parents, a role which she would follow into as she came out of school - [[The Flash]] struck Jinny hard when her parents were lost to it. She joined the EDA, and was taken in by the Foreshadow taskforce, all of whom took a mentor-ish role with her but the standout was Dread-Man, the leader of the team and the person for whom she was considered the side-kick to.

When the story began she was transferred to the Sentinels, viewed as a known quantity who could be expected to act in the Agency’s best interests without compromising the mission or endangering civilians. She also was selected because her time with the Foreshadow taskforce gave the impression that she would have the willingness to take on a darker turn for the agency if needed, a point which was never tested but likely would not have played out in the agency’s favour.

Jinny has never distinguished herself as someone very socially capable, having a lot of anxiety even up to the very end as to how effective of a leader she was to her team, it became very clear to their handler that she was fiercely loyal to them, and if the agency had wished to use her to influence them in some malicious way she would firmly rebuffed it.

Jinny’s position with the team shares a lot of similarities with Herman’s, often being sources of information gathering or a tool belt to help deal with issues and figure out mysteries. Yet she came to show herself as exceptionally powerful, her telepathic abilities quickly breaking the bounds of simply affecting another’s mind to melting metal to reviving a brain for one last conversation.

Her defining moment, or moments, would be reaction to the trauma that she has been subjected to. The loss of her mentors, or rather, her family and then the loss of her brother all came in short order. Yet, despite her personal feelings about always being on the brink of collapse, she held firm and trudged on. Revealing herself to be powerful of mind, not only in the sense of her powers, but of an iron will that has yet to be budged, let alone broken.

#### Doctor Herman Andreas Fitzgerald

A Chaotic Adept who Masters the Swarm
Played by Hoke

[[Doctor Herman Andreas Fitzgerald]], known ostensibly to the public as ‘Doctor Bees’, is truly only ever called Herman by the team. A man of science his whole life, Herman found himself in the position of an engineer and scientist working to assist heroes with gadgets, equipment and research at [[Bastion Bio-engineering Inc.]]

During his tenure, a lab accident left him with a peculiar mutation - when his body took in the venom or DNA of an insect, he would mutate to take on physical characteristics of said insect. On top of that, he gained the ability to manifest swarms and even varying levels of communications with the bugs. However he remained working as a scientist, not yet taking up the mantle of a hero. 

After [[The Flash]], his company fell apart and he was left without direction until the EDA took note of him and got him to join the team. Despite being the obvious candidate for leadership, being the most senior in age and experience in the “superhero field,” he was seconded in favour of Jinny - a point he had little issue with.

While working with the team he served as the logical backbone, having a hand in most fights by aiding others as well as his own moments of visceral violence before returning to his own quips. His effectiveness on the battlefield continued to grow once he gained his sidekick Edelweiss, a butterfly who later came into position of a super suit which was a key asset in many fights.

Herman’s most notable pressure on the story is felt in the double of his made by the [[King in Yellow]] which was a horrific looking bugman of similar powers but lacking (even for King in Yellow creations) a startling amount of humanity. It would come to be revealed that Herman was in fact the King in Yellows deepest preference for someone to turn to his side. However Herman was a tough nut to crack, and had rebuffed every effort - consciously or not - of being influenced by the King in Yellow.

At the end of the story, when they had to make their way into [[Carcosa]], Herman accepted the last weight put on him - of having to (as far as he knew) permanently take on buglike features so that he might match the cosmic signature of his bugman double. He proudly did so, add this self sacrifice to the long list of efforts he had contributed to the Sentinels’ battle against the King in Yellow.

#### Matthew Cooper

An Amazing Warrior who Moves like the Wind
Played by Jigurd

The newest member to the Sentinel team, [[Matt Cooper]] is from the universe of [[Earth 836]], sharing an origin with the member who left the team before him, [[Aura]]. Matt Cooper’s Earth is one of a more cyberpunk persuasion, with some but not great representations of Superheroes as they are on [[Earth 101]].

He responded to an ad for testing, in return for substantial cash, of a new drug that promised to improve athletic capabilities. He was given an injection, had an exo-suit biocoded to him and then began the initial tests, where he was simply tasked with jogging on a treadmill. The jog became a sprint, which became a run, which became a sprint, and then finally it became a dimensional jump.

Ending up now in Earth 101, he was quickly collected by the Other Earthers and given lodgings in Emerald City and given the job that Aura had just vacated - as he now was a better fit for it and she had better uses elsewhere.

Initially selected by Director Lane for the purpose of being able to contain Kali if she went rogue, Matt has distinguished himself as the right arm to Kali’s left in fights and a source of humour and banter for the team, with a good heart.

His destiny double is [[Bluebolt]], who later became wrapped up in the King in Yellow’s plans, but his two allies - the [[Mayweather Mystic]] and [[Nightshade]] - became Matt’s as well. His actual double in this dimension, however, did not give him an initial warm welcome, though they had evened out towards the end of the campaign.

The greatest incident for Matt Cooper, and what he would be going down in the history of V&T for, would be his efforts in facing [[White Flag]]. In their first engagement, Matt Cooper took his hand and called in his [[Other Earther]] backup to end the fight. White Flag then used his power to try and have others take Matt Cooper’s hand in return, but only succeeded in painful scarring it. 

In their final confrontation, they both struck blows upon the other - and White Flag did finally succeed in taking Matt’s hand - that would be the only victory the fallen hero would be given. As after Matt rose, he took down White Flag once and for all, leaving his acidic blood pooling body to melt into the ground beneath him. In the battle with the King in Yellow himself, Matt caused many a wound upon the cosmic being and suffered even more himself.

#### Aura

An Impulsive Explorer who Sculpts Hard Light
Played by Bogi

The briefest member of the Sentinel team, and one of who’s canonicity was initially in question, Aura is from the universe of [[Earth 836]], but more specifically she is the cosmic being associated with that Earth’s moon. In cosmic terms, she was quite young. Not a child, but far from a very accomplished adult. 

A cosmic being from her reality, seeing that the [[Zombie Virus]] had already laid its claws on this dimension, decided to take pity on Aura and sent her to [[Earth 101]] (the V&T dimension). Once on the other side, she was contacted by the [[Other Earthers]] and given a position with EDA (which would shortly become [[AEGIS]].)

She served on one mission with the team, assisting them in investigating the dinosaur island but once the mission was over she was transferred to a different piece of the EDA, where she was quietly dropped from membership by an Other Earthers’ agent and used as an asset off world. 

There is no firm canonicity for what happened to her after, but I am inclined to say that until she shows up again, she is relatively living happily ever after.
