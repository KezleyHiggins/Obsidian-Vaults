#GoodnightCalifornia

# Origins & Concept

This campaign, originally simply named just “[[Vigilantes & Tragedies]],” had the suffix “[[Goodnight, California]]” added to the title since the second campaign with the Thursday group began. The want to play it came from a random impulse one day while waiting for Jasmin to return to her apartment in Claremont. I knew that I wanted to run something again and after a off-the-cuff decision based on two YouTube videos, I decided to go with the Cypher system and instantly fell in love. I picked the genre being superheroes since I hadn't run something in that for some time.

The setting's inspiration came from taking the premise of the MCU's Snap and treating as something that had stuck with the world, and had its severity turned up a bit. This incident is called "[[The Flash]]." A hero called [[Vanguard]] had gone crazy, left earth, taken control of a cosmic being and then returned to release a wave of energy that washed over the planet and killed 60% of all human life, prioritising those who were more powerful. This effect spread out and away from the Earth, effecting the whole universe. 

[[Goodnight, California]] is set one year after the Flash, where the [[Earth Defence Agency]] - this setting's SHIELD analogue - had it's American branch take a heel-turn away from the broader agency and begin a public facing team. This new team, called the [[Sentinels]], was sponsored by a former top-star superhero from this setting's version of the Avengers - a man called [[Maximillian Mars]], better known as the Commander, and former member of the [[Titans]].

The initial adventure that it began with was based off of my premise for game called [[Dead Super Tells All]], which was about a Superhero stuck on an island fighting dinosaurs. This then became the backstory and premise for the initial mission, where the party were being sent to an island that the [[Earth Defence Agency|EDA]], before their heel-turn into the [[Sentinels]], sends the team to confirm the status of a [[Khano Jakoba|supervillain]] who was known for hunting down and killing heroes.

After that, I came up with the idea of intermingling the idea of the King in Yellow from the Arkham Horror TCG that Jigurd and I played, including interlocking our characters into the story (Mark Harrigan and Joe Diamond) and from that point the story came. 

I pulled from many influences, including the Paragons setting from Mutants and Masterminds, some pieces of the Claim the Sky setting from Monte Cook Games, and heaps of premises and ideas from other comic sources that would be much more difficult to condense into a list.

## Reflections
### What Went Well

* The world is internally consistent, through and through. 
* The world gave good and inclusive space for the party to make characters that felt unique and theirs
* Everyone was invested in each other's characters
* The story was a genuine mystery the whole way through, with the PCs having new leads to follow but never being able to really guess the next steps.
* The start to the campaign was very strong, since it easily tanked Bogi being there for the first few then leaving afterwards, then Jigurd joining later.

### What Went Poorly

* There was along time during this game where I definitely was stumbling through some of the rules more than I was actually smoothly playing. We did eventually get better at it, and nowadays I have the system basically grokked, this was a definitely point that could have been improved, even though being honest the only way to get here is to play, I'm still mentioning it.
* I have it written in my original notes that this campaign did suffer at times from the usual pains of there being gaps in play that resulted in the dreaded **"wait, why are we doing this?"** feeling creeping in. I think that this is something I will need to analyze further myself to find ways to solve it, since it is a general pain that I've found very demotivating but it's a reality of running these games as an adult.
* I think that to run Cypher long-term you should offer things, in universe, that give people new things to spend XP on, and get XP from, in order to help guide play and make your table feel like a separate game. I have it in my notes that I wished I did this better, and it is broadly something that I still wish that I'd found a way to do it.
* I also don't think that I leant in enough to giving people a moment to be in the Spotlight as just themselves, being a unique hero with just the powers to fix this problem. I think that I should have tried harder to give people their Spidey stopping the train moment.

### What To Carry Forward

**The Setting**

It is great, carries a lot of weight with everything that happens and can cater to basically any kind of superhero story that I would want to tell or that my players would like to have included. I still think so now, even though the setting is about four or so years old. It was created around May of 2022.

I also think that all the confidence and feelings I have of making something that is a unique, auteur product by me is something I need to trust. The people I play with know that I cook hard, so I gotta make sure that I perform in the kitchen for them.

### What To Improve On

- I have it noted that I need to get better with the rules, but that has basically been achieved, yet it was worth noting at the time of the post mortem so I'll keep it noted here.
- Gaps between sessions resulting in dropped subplots
	- Take better and more diligent notes during and after sessions to ensure that I don't miss any information. This includes keeping track of subplots, both as a list and potentially using something like Blades In The Dark clocks.
	- I also suggest the idea of writing session summaries, but I think that what really I should do is reward players for writing the summaries as Joe has in his games to create that culture for it.
- I have a note about not giving enough cyphers, which I no longer agree with, but I'm just noting it down because at the time I apparently felt it was worth noting. Broadly, I think that they're a very optional piece of content that should just be used as one-time items when needed.
- Not enough incentive to use XP on benefits
- During Cypher I need to try and make the feeling of managing resources to increase tension something that is an actual focus in prep, in order to help raise the stakes.
- Give people moments to have their powers shine in a very outright "this is your time" moment.
- I have a note about the "cast of NPCs being too large or being forgotten," but I think this is just down to trying to be better about finding art for people and doing things to more easily remind players of them.
