# Lore

[[The Party#Jigurd Skint|Skint]] asked [[Barcombe#Important NPCs|Richter]] to help them try and figure out what may be happening. As they've now alerted the town to the greater danger that it's in through their discoveries the priest has agreed to work through the night to create a short list for them, but since he's working off of imperfect information he can only give them a broad recap of his evidence.

**The recap:**

Adventurers, I have spent all time since you left until the hours of dusk going through all the tomes and cyclopedias in the town's collection to find candidates for the threat that stands before us. Unfortunately, as I said in person, a concrete determination has been impossible but I do firmly believe that the source must be one of the below. 

The description of the state of mind of the Goblins being altered, and their behaviour seemingly controlled, does not narrow down the focus but the shared feature of the wound over the heart has helped.

- Necromancy
	- Perhaps an obvious revelation considering the dangers that lie north of us all, but in the wake of the Oldtower's master has left many opportunities for those practicing his craft under him to aspire to their own villainy.
- A Powerful Artifact
	- A broader answer than you may wish, but this level of magic - bearing such a personalized flare - is often sourced from an artifact that has the means to perform such magic "easily" for the user. Controlling the minds of those infected in their blood and heart by you is no minor feat to be sure, but if one were well equipped with a [[Magic Swords|Magic Sword]] like that of the Canonical Eight, even their minor cousins like that of [[Anthea|Anthea's]] City Keepers, [[Mistveil|Mistveil's]] Warding Weapons or the many blades of [[Gallant|Gallant's]] heritages.
- A living plague
	- A frightful answer to consider, but one that bears keeping in mind. The uniformity of the "infection" and the effects on the mind is not unlike that of the plague sent against [[Glasghoul]] all those years ago. It too was thought to be simply a spell that could be struck down, and was discovered too late to be a plague, albeit a magical one.

## The Landscape

Heading east from Barcombe, the landscape is hilly and uneven, with it sometimes feeling closer to crossing a dune than walking up a grassy hill. The soil has been left soft and unmoored after the trees were cut down by the Dwarves of the [[Hilltop Fortress]].

Assuming they travel with focus, meaning that they're essentially spending the whole day travelling minus the space in this nebulous time to handle one random encounter should it trigger, then making their way between each point on the Point Crawl is going to take a day.

Every time they travel, one of them rolls 2d6. A 1 on the primary die means that whatever the encounter is, is some kind of danger. 

###### Point A

When the players come to a stop after their adventuring, they find a nice spot by a hill that allows them to sleep in a place that's shaded by trees and the surrounding land. Everyone rolls perception, highest one finds the following:

A door forged from Bronze metal set into the side of the hill, like a hobbit mound. You need to spread some aggressively growing weeds and foliage apart to see it, and though its not really set up to be an appropriate height for you all to be very comfy, it can be slept inside and easily accommodate all three of the party.

Opening the door requires one of the few pieces of culture that the company would have taught the three, and the inside has carvings and pieces of story telling and references that does not make any sense nor map onto any context the party would know. There are many symbols that they know are to signify information about the area past language barriers, which unsurprisingly tell the party that there are Manticores in the area.

###### Point B

Evidence of a Kobold migration camp moving ahead. Investigation checks to get new pieces of information required, but it seems that a band of about fifty-ish kobolds all moving in a disorganized group. There's a center mass with the rest of them all seeming to be moving ahead towards the Hilltop.

###### Point C

The party finds the All-Eater, in camp, a giant kobold eating and sloshing down upon the barrels that they found in the tomb. He is gigantic, the largest kobold any of them have ever seen, and were they not laying their own eyes on him, the description of them would beggar belief.
