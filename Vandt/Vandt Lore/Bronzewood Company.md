The Bronzewood Company is an ancient [[Freebooter Caravan]] that has a reputation for being good at their jobs, getting involved in other caravan's business too often and perceived as breaking tradition as far as who they let join.

The core values of the caravan are that they always seek to "do the hero's work," and "make the caravan's due." Their symbol is a [[Bronzewood]] tree, from which they take their name. Their colours are green, black and bronze.

The Company is ancient and sprawling, rarely ever all together but more often spread out into smaller groupings referred to as branches. The company considers all [[Bronzewood|Bronzewood Trees]] their property and important to them culturally. Many cities or holdings that have Bronzewood Trees technically have that land owned solely by the company, like in [[Anthea]].

###### Structure and Practices:

**How are decisions made within the caravan?**
	Takes notes from a Motorcycle Club structure
	Decisions, when available to be made, are made through votes 
	There are people with roles who can take precedence over specific decisions (eg fighting captain making a call in a fight)

**What are the roles and responsibilities of different members?** 
	Doctor 
	Quartermaster

**What are your caravan's customs and traditions? Do you have any rituals or celebrations that are unique to your group?**
	Caravan’s own version of thieves’ cant
	Scheduled Church-style meeting
	Plant a Bronzewood  tree at camping grounds
	Green leaves during summer, black during winter, no snow keeps on the trees
	Myth about it being protective, part rumour
	Takes knickknacks from different campgrounds where trees have been planted
	You make your own wagons for the caravan

**How do you recruit new members? What qualities are you looking for in potential Freebooters?**
	Through a unanimous vote they get entered into a prospect status and then they get full admission

###### Culture and Beliefs:

**What are your religious beliefs, if any?**
Caravan isn’t institutionally religious, but members are free to practise.

**What are the taboos and social norms within your caravan?**
	People pretending to be part of the caravan is a massive no-no and dealt with swiftly.
	Every member respects the decision of the company. 
	Seen as a taboo to hold it against someone for their vote, everyone is seen as having the company’s best interest at heart.
	Kids of company members go through a different route to earn their vote, they have a half membership situation until they can vote.
