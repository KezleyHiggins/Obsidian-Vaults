This game takes place in the world of **Vandt**. It is a world with medieval technology and high fantasy elements. There are humans, elves, undead, wizards, gods, spirits, magic swords and prophecies.

The story is focused on the southern-most tip of Vandt, called the **Marches**. It includes everything south of the **Oldtower**, and also refers to the broad alliance that the three main states had formed. The largest threat of the world, the **Necromancer** of the **Oldtower**, disappeared some thirty years ago and no one knows why or where he went, or more worryingly if he's ever coming back.

# The Party

Each of the player characters are, first of all, people who want to be heroes. You're all on the younger side (under thirty) and though you have had enough experience and training to earn you whatever abilities your class gives you at level one, you are unambiguously new adventurers.

You're all joined by your shared desire to join the **Bronzewood Company**. The specifics about the company are explained below, but the short version is that they're an ancient **Freebooter Caravan**, which is a type of mercenary company in this world that acts as its own moving city or culture. The Bronzewood Company is known for their two directives being **do the heroes work**, and get **the company's due**.

The sigil of the company is a Bronzewood tree on a split shield of black and green. As such, the colours of the company are green, black and bronze. The party each bear a token from the company that can be used to prove their position as a prospect of the company, and likely also have been adorned with clothing to further show their membership.

The party has been sent to the town of **Barcombe**. They have been told that the town has reached out for help from the company to help combat its local issues, and this has been seen as a small enough issue to be worthy of you three being sent to deal with it. 

# The Local Area

The village of Barcombe sits between the borders of **Gallant** and **Mistveil**, though apparently is not part of either state. It is populated mainly with humans and dwarves. It also features its own Bronzewood tree - which is something that company always acts to protect no matter where in the world and is likely why this village was able to ask for help from the company.

# The Danger

**Oldtower:** This refers to the largest building on the face of Vandt, a huge imposing necromancer's tower that is taller than any mountain. From nearly anywhere in the Marches, you can see it poking over the horizon. A huge swathe of land, called the **Wastes**, surrounds it and extends far up north and it's southern-tip meets a river which has halted its advancement further south.

**The Necromancer:** This figure was the greatest evil the world had ever known. His name has even been lost to time, but while other people may have also used magic to wake the dead, he was *the* Necromancer. All peoples on Vandt have had some run-in with his attempts to take over the continent.

**Wastes:** The landscape around the tower is rotted and sickly, even just walking close to the area can make you feel ill. This was one of the defensive measures taken by the Necromancer to avoid those who oppose him marching armies up to siege his tower. 

# The Politics

**The Marcher Alliance:** The three main states in the Marches are **Mistveil**, **Anthea** and **Gallant**. The alliance was formed in order to have them all survive against the efforts of the evil Necromancer to take them over. It enforced diplomatic ties, normalized relations and also standardized the highest titles between all the states to be Marquesses.

**Mistveil:** The city-state of Mistveil claims the center territories of the Marches. The landscape is defined by inherent magics that cause anything magical to generate a mist, or even a fog if the influence is strong enough. It is settled by the **High Elves**, but main peoples include **Constructs**, **Glasskin Elves**, **Oozelings** and **Humans**. It is a republic, though due to the Marcher Alliance the highest office is also always the **Marquess**.

**Anthea:** The Kingdom of Anthea is ruled by a court of vampires. The Blood-King is the name of the title and is held by **Diomedes**. He has several vampires that he has turned and occupy the other high offices who all have the title of **Lord Husband** or **Lady Wife**. Despite the rulers being literal vampires, the long-life of all those in office has lead to a sprawling city that is ever growing and consistent laws.

The creation of new whole vampires is forbidden except with the Blood-King's permission, however some vampires have had children which has resulted in Vampirekin, humans with pale skin, black hair, fangs and the ability to harmlessly ingest blood among some other variations.

The main peoples of Anthea are **Dwarves**, **Humans** (the two dominant cultures being the Ancient Greek inspired **Antheans** and Ancient Egyptian inspired **Altans**), **Vampirekin**, **Ratfolk** and **Oozelings**.

**Gallant:** The Duchy of Gallant contains many different noble titles beneath it and has been the most active in combating the Necromancer. This also means that it has seen the most bloodshed and thus also been the most aggressive in assimilating new peoples into itself in order to keep it from having its population dwindle.

It is the religious center of the Marcher Alliance, featuring the most churches and the largest cathedral. All available ancestries to the players have some prominence in Gallant and the state has made efforts to integrate them into the culture, but the dominant peoples remain **Humans** and **Dwarves**.

# Religion

Gods are real in the world of Vandt, as are spirits. Though many gods share vastly different manners of worshiping them and their own spheres of control and interest, the uniting factor between them is that if a god exists, it's constellation can be found in the stars. The stars don't change or shift at night in Vandt, no matter where you are.

The only changes are with gods with many aspects or faces - such as the god of Vigilance and Tragedy, whose constellation changes to reflect which aspect of them is presently looking down upon the world. 

Spirits, magical beings that have their swathe of magical influence but don't have a constellation, also exist and are often focuses of religion or culture. An example would be the **Sandman**, who is worshipped by all Elves. Elves, coming originally from the **Fey** realm of **Arcadia**, don't sleep in their home realm. This lack of ability to sleep themselves means that elves in the material plane of Vandt will literally die of exhaustion from being unable to sleep - however this is avoided from the **Sandman**, a spirit, who individually comes to each of them to deliver upon them sleep and provide them dreams.
