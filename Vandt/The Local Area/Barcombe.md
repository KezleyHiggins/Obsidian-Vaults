 Barcombe is a village to the north of Mistveil and Gallant, though it is unaligned due to its connection with the Bronzewood Company. 
 
###### History

The town is old, as most towns are, and has a strong sense of independence from the people that live there. The Bronzewood Tree that the village has within its walls is well cared for and maintained, and that has led to them receiving protection from the Company in exchange for the tree being cared for.

This relationship grew to include the Company leveraging this relationship to have the village create supplies, lodge members, and so on. It's not formally a member of the Company, but it's as close as one could be.

###### The Mayoral Mansion

Though called a mansion by those within the town, it is far closer to a small keep. It is bounded by stone walls and simple battlements, but is otherwise open to all members of the village to come and go. The Sheriff's office and the town watch are also based out of this building. 

If the town is ever under threat, this is where everyone flees back to.

###### The Church of His Vigilance and Her Tragedy

This is the church run by Richter. It is consecrated to the god of Vigilance and Tragedy, but as with all churches it has the means for others to pray to and worship the other gods if they wish. In the roof of the church is an observatory and book on finding the different constellations. 

He is an adept healer, who largely wants the town to go back to being left alone by all forces outside of them.

###### The Beerwood Inn

This is an impressively big inn that is the sole establishment of its kind for the town, which was an edict voted on by the town, so that issues with adventurers or drunkenness could be centralized to one place that would then be easier to police.

It run by Matron, a construct. She is very friendly and warm, and enjoys being able to help out the adventurers as much as she can.

###### Nimneri's Alchemy

This is a store and alchemy lab run by an Altan woman called Nimneri. She is interested in business beyond all, seeking to get involved in any commerce in the town. She is the richest member by far, as her preparations are used by basically everyone in the town.

###### The Rooster's Smithy

A former member of a dwarven freebooter company, the Rooster is missing his left foot (which appropriately has been replaced by a metal chicken foot as a replacement) and has a shock of bright red hair.

He is known to be quite kind under all his bluster and anger, often helping out the town and accepting whatever they might be able to spare and happily extending debts as far as needed.

###### The General Store

This is a store that is owned by Nimneri, but is run at an exact match for whatever it buys, serving as a place for locals to buy things that they'd need or otherwise need to argue with outsider traders for.
