
**Academy Dropout**
You studied at a magical institution of some sort before joining the company, whether that be a formal school or just under a mentor. Sometimes, school just isn’t for everyone! You learn by experience in the real world (or at least that’s what you tell yourself). Learn any 1 Utility Spell.

**Ear to the Ground**
Your life was defined by being able to get information on those around you and take advantage of it. Perhaps as a member of a noble court, or a spy, or simply a criminal selling information. Advantage on checks to know or obtain gossip for events that will soon happen or have happened less than 1 year ago.

**Well Travelled**
Before joining the company, you followed a profession that had you travel across the Marches, potentially as a merchant or scam-artist. Whatever it is, it's left a wide variance for the type of peoples that you know.

1/per location (or at the GM’s discretion). You happen to know JUST the person who has the information you’re looking for, or could get you out of a jam, and… Roll 1d20:
	1–5. They want you DEAD.
	6–12. You owe them money.
	13–19. They can be convinced to help you..
	20. They are your biggest fan/are madly in love with you.

**Wanderer**
You lived out and amongst the wilds before joining the company, perhaps as some form of religious exploration or just your character wishing to be closer to nature. Whether it is the sticks or flowers in your hair, your smell, or the way you carry yourself, wild creatures are less frightened of you and more willing to aid you. +1 Naturecraft. While Field Resting, roll your Hit Dice with advantage while in the wild.

**Hermit**
Your life before joining the company significantly relied on you being able to live off the land, likely as a ranger, hunter or poacher. You never run out of your own personal rations. Anything can be food if you try hard enough! Advantage against poison saves. +1 max Hit Die.

**Scholar**
You studied in your days before joining the company. Advantage on all Lore checks related to knowledge about items, facts, or events that happened more than 100 years ago.

**Noble**
You are some form of nobility, appropriate to your ancestry and where you wish to be from. You always have up-to-date knowledge of the customs and dress of the upper classes and may even know many of their secrets. Advantage on Influence checks with the upper class.

# Tradesmen

**Tradesman/Artisan**
Choose a profession (Baker/Cook, Smith, Stonemason, Weaver, Leatherworker, etc.). Checks you make related to that profession are made with advantage. You also retain special knowledge related to your profession.

Ask your GM if your profession and Ancestry background will also entail knowing a language.

###### Altan Alchemist
You served as an apprentice to an Altan Alchemist in Anthea. This involves knowing how to use potions and especially snake venom for healing. Checks you make related to this profession are made with advantage.

If you're Human, Dragonborn, Oozeling or Ratfolk, you know Draconic.

###### Dwarven Smith
You served as an apprentice to a true Dwarven smith, learning the ancient methods of how they turn rough clumps of rock and stone into mythical weapons, armours and works of art. Checks you make related to this profession are made with advantage.

If you're Dungeonfolk or a Dwarf, you know Dwarvish.

###### Glazier Smith
You served as an apprentice to a Glazier smith, learning the process of how to process raw sand into magically resistant glass which can be hard enough to match steel developed by the Glasskin whilst still inside the Oldtower. Checks you make related to this profession are made with advantage.

If you're a High Elf, Glasskin Elf, Construct or Oozeling then you also know Elvish.

**Raised by Goblins**
You speak Goblin natively (much better than one who has learned it later in life). You automatically notice and can avoid crudely-made traps and have advantage to notice and disarm more sophisticated traps.
