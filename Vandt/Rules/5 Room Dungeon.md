
The [5 Room Dungeon](https://www.roleplayingtips.com/5-room-dungeons/) is a story structure designed to help make segments of content, not specifically dungeons, but pieces of an adventure that can be split into five different pieces in order to supply a pleasing narrative arc.

They are, as the name suggests, split into five separate rooms.

#### Room One
**Entrance & Guardian**

There needs to be a reason why your dungeon hasn't been plundered before and/or why the PCs are the heroes for the job. This is a guardian or challenge right at the entrance which serves as the justification for why this place remains untouched, hidden or merely intact.

The guardian also can provide some early action to capture player interest and energize a session. Room one also presents an opportunity to establish the mood and theme of the dungeon.

**Challenge Ideas:**
- The entrance is trapped.
- The entrance is cleverly hidden.
- The entrance requires a special key, like a ceremony or command word.
- The guardian is something deliberately placed to keep people out, like a golem.
- A dangerous creature has made room one it's lair, but isn't indigenous so it doesn't go inside.

#### Room Two
**Puzzle Or Roleplaying Challenge**

The PCs are victorious over the challenge of the first room and are now presented with a trial that cannot also be resolved with steel. This keeps the problem solvers in your group happy and breaks the action up for good pacing.

This room should be a puzzle, skill-based or roleplaying encounter if possible. 

**If Room One was this type of encounter, then this should be combat oriented instead.***

**Challenge Ideas:**
- Magic puzzle, like a chess board.
- A spirit or ghost that must be convinced to let the party pass without raising the alarm.
- A group of creatures like bats or birds that can be spooked which would let the rest of dungeon know they're there.

#### Room Three
**Trick Or Setback**

This third room is here to build tension. This is done using a trick, trap or setback. For example, after defeating a tough monster, the players think they've finally found the treasure and achieved their goal, they learn that the room is actually a false end.

This should subvert expectations and/or make things more complicated. This could be something like what they thought was the boss actually was a mini-boss, or that the thing they've come to find *is* here but is broken or otherwise different than they were lead to believe.

**Challenge Ideas:**
- One way exit (that would force them to have to go through the first two rooms again).
- The artifact they were hunting is found, but is broken, cursed or has pieces missing with clues to where to look next.
- Some worrying information that the quest they're following along isn't as simple as it first seemed.

#### Room Four
**Climax, Big Battle, or Conflict**

This room is the main event. It's the final combat or conflict within the dungeon. Use all the tactics you can summon to make this encounter memorable and entertaining. This can be done via interesting terrain, interactive monsters or a neat boss.

Start or end with roleplay. This could be the bad guy trying to stall the PCs at the beginning for his help to arrive, or after they've been downed and bleeding out they give their last few words to the PCs. The leftover hostages or minions also could be the source of the roleplay.

**Challenge Ideas:**
- The bad guy has had some manner of knowing that the PCs are coming that they didn't expect.
- The bad guy offers to settle things in an unusual way, such as through a wager or duel.
- The lair is trapped, but the bad guy knows how to avoid it, or can trip them to affect the PCs.
- The bad guys threaten to break whatever the PCs are after to try and get them to leave.

#### Room Five
**Reward, Revelation, Plot Twist**

This is the opportunity to change the players' bragging to "we came, we saw, we slipped on a banana peel." This doesn't always have to represent a complication or point of failure for the PCs, but it can. This room doesn't always need to be a physical location either, it can be a twist revealed in room four.

This room should be where creativity shines and what makes this dungeon different and memorable from the others in your campaigns. If there hasn't been a reward yet for conquering the dungeon, now would be a good place to put it, whether its gold, magic items or what the PCs were looking for.

**Challenge Ideas:**
- Information about the bad guy is revealed.
- New clues or plot information are revealed.
- A piece of bonus treasure that might point the PCs to a new dungeon.
