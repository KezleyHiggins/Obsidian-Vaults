###### 1. Can an Android Truly Feel? by RatchetMechani
Story follows the 'CPU King' and an attendant discussing why life matters, before being attacked by Havoc Marines and then being killed for their hubris. In their final moments, they feel emotions.

**Review: 2/5**
I didn't hate their interpretation of the Robot Legions, but it felt very contrived for them to reference what they did. There's a nugget of a good idea of robots arguing about why life matters before falling prey to their pride, but is rushed through here.

###### 2. Mind The Impossible by arcanelviejo
Story follows an officer trying to communicate via radio to an unspecified (though presumably robotic) unit that appears to be attaining sentience? It's not really clear.

**Review: 2/5**
I didn't really enjoy the story, but I like the attempt to try and make the user constantly question who's speaking through the use of the font. This felt difficult to read in spite of it's premise not because of it.

###### 3. The First Extraction by Kaiga Hobbies
This covers the researcher in charge of the creation of the Grimdark Future Ratmen and them escaping a facility with the 'First Ratman.'

**Review: 3/5**
I really love the premise of this story, but it is hurt by going into a very wordy pay-off similar to that of Caesar in Planet of the Apes escaping but without any of the appropriate build up. If the writing flowed better and used paragraphs more to build in some context to make things hit harder it would be higher, but the separate sentences really hurts the flow.

###### 5. Another Shot by Royal Youngs
Follows a rookie space marine crashlanding in a ship and dying while charging the enemy and having a reality check on themselves as they die slowly. 

**Review: 4/5**
Really good story, the execution could use some work, as its stilted in some places. I think if this was refined more, didn't repeat words (in a negative fashion) as much it would be stronger. I also think that I like the somewhat comedically one note portrayal of the space marines doesn't match the interior monologue's tone.

###### 6. An Apple, Unremembered by jhontor

This is about an Ossified Dead lady who is tending a garden having a conversation with a human who comes to collect apples. She shows compassion to the girl and lets her go, but ultimately does give way to the control of the lord who controls her and keeps the humans away and refuses the reciprocated gift.

**Review: 5/5**
Genuinely a great story. The only complaints are some turns of phrase that wrinkled the brow more than they did evoke an image, but the story is extremely unique to the setting and genuinely made me care about the Ossified Dead.

###### 7. THE INERADICABLE IGNIS by Muhammad Islam

Follows a person in service to the angels who opens a tomb and reads a tablet that reveals that his ancestors were killed by the angels and he is a slave, but eventually succumbs to the influence of the angels.

**Review: 4/5**
This story has a fun premise and some good lines of writing, but the manner in which the angels speak and the confusing jumps in where everyone is and what's happening takes away from the enjoyment of what should probably have just been from them unmoving in one place.

###### 8. The Blank Verse by iskerz_studio

This is about a frog-mage from the Saurian Starhost coming to a pyramid in order to awaken some frog-mages but has ongoing feelings of something being wrong and his readings not laying on exactly what was happening. He is confronted with a manifestation of *nothing* inside there, which he can't explain.

**Review: 5/5**
Great story that executes on the fantasy promised by the Saurian Starhost. The only complaint I have is that compared to an An Apple, Unremembered is that the emotional hooks don't land as deep, but this is very good.

###### 9. The Saint Who Could Not Be Saved by yuika

Follows a machine cult saint being turned into a full robot and the worries over him actually surviving, before revealing that he did not survive it in the end.

**Review: 5/5**
Great story that humanizes the machine cult really well. There are a few lines that just read a little off to me but is otherwise super solid. You're invested and the dilemma is very interesting without beating you over the head with it. 

###### 10. THE BRIDGE OF DUNMARCH by Colton Haning

**Review: 4/5**

This isn't a bad story in terms of plot, but the formatting made it a chore to read due to the large paragraphs. It also doesn't really follow "impossible" very much as a theme.

###### 12. Fool’s Gold by xwingtavernwars

Follows two space dwarves who went going for gold and ended up stuck because the stupider of the two of them fired an explosive that caused a cave-in. Their plan was to sneak away with the gold to become rich, but now if they're found they'll be killed for going against their guild. If they're not found, they die. They resolve to try and blast their way out.

**Review: 4/5**
Fun premise, fun characters, integrates the theme nicely. Let down by some of the writing being a bit one note and missing punctuation, could have done with maybe making you care a bit more about them by either being more heartfelt or funnier.
