This mercifully means that the notion of being trained or untrained in weapons is no longer present. Additionally, there is also an equipment bundle (that can be altered, as above mentioning that the weapon can be swapped out.)

It also means that **all** the abilities specific to their type are gained at Tier 1.

### Foci

Foci are very different and are now central - really the *meat* of your character's class. The idea that multiple people could be one Type being permissible remains, even though now it's less likely just due to the fact that there are so many.

![[Pasted image 20260621235539.png]]

When you pick a Focus, it's no longer an abstract bucket. Now, it's a (potentially) mutually exclusive tree of decisions you can make. Picking options is far less of just "pick what you want" - which I think is good, it makes it feel more like this is what you are rather than its just flavoured this way.

![[Pasted image 20260621235957.png]]

### Skills

Characters now have Background skills which means that a PC no longer need to expend a special ability slot to get access to skills that would represent knowledge from their backstory.

They also have further clarified how someone gaining a skill in something to do with Combat works, meaning they can get become *trained* beginning at tier 2, and *specialized* at tier 4.
